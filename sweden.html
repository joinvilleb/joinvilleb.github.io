Introduction & Geography

Location

Sweden is a country that is located in Northern Europe, bordering the Baltic Sea, and is between Finland and Norway. Stockholm is the capital of Sweden. The country had a significant role during World War II, which you can learn more about at this Wikipedia page.

Climate and Terrain

Sweden is a large country, which means that the northern and southern points experience huge shifts in climate. Northern Sweden experiences subartic temperatures. Probably not the best place to go if you are wanting to spend time outdoors! And, in southern Sweden, you experience cold, cloudy winters and cool, partly cloudy summers.

Area and comparative area to the United States

The size of Sweden could be compared to three times the size of Georgia of the United States. The total area is 450,295 sq km with a total land area of 410,335 sq km. The country ranks the 58th largest country in the world.

People and Society

Demographic profile

This is an interesting section as it is quite different from the United States. Swedish society is very gender equal, and men and women in the country agree that both parners should contribute to household income (which is how it should be).

There is a generous leave policy and high-quality subsidized childcare which allows mothers and fathers to balance work and family life. In fact, Swedish women have one of the highest labor participation rates in Europe, and one if its highest total fertility rates. Beginning in 2008, Sweden began a new era of labor immigration as companies were encouraged to hire non-EU workers. Not surprising, but as of 2020, over a quarter of Sweden's population had a migrant background.

Population distribution

The majority of people who are in Sweden live in the South. There are population clusters found all along the Baltic coast in the east; the interior areas of the north remain sparsely populated. The current population of Stockholm, Sweden (the capital) is approx. 1.7 million out of the entire country as of 2023.

Government

Constitution

Sweden has four fundamental laws which together make up the Constititon and were adopted in 1991. These four are:

  • The Instrument of Government
  • The Act of Succession
  • The Freedom of the Press Act
  • The Fundamental Law on Freedom of Expression

There are also amendemnts to the constitution. Any amendments sought to be made to the constitution are first proposed by Parliament.

Branches of government

There are three branches of government in Sweden. They are the Executive branch, the Legislative Branch, and the Judicial branch.

Branch of government Description
Executive branch The chief of state currently, is King CARL XVI GUSTAF, since 1973. The cabinet is appointed by the prime minister.
Legislative branch this is a unicameral Parliament (or Riksdag) which has 349 seats; 310 members are directly elected in multi-seat constituencies. Members of the legislative branch serve four (4) terms. The last election was held on 11 September 2022.
Judicial branch There are 2 high courts. One is the Supreme Court of Sweden (which consists of 16 justices, including one court chairman).

Citizenship

There is no citizenship by birth, which is different from the United States. In Sweden, you are able to become a citizen by descent ONLY. If there is a case where a child is born out of wedlock, the moether must be a citizen of Sweden and the father unknown.
